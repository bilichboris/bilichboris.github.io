In 1970, Joseph L. Taylor introduced the notion of the joint spectrum of a tuple of commuting operators on a Banach space. He constructed the holomorphic functional calculus in the neighborhood of the spectrum and proved the spectral mapping theorem. This theory can be easily extended to representations of any commutative algebra on a Banach space. Later, there were several attempts to generalize this theory to noncommutative algebras. In many of these attempts, the main focus was on formulating and proving the appropriate version of the spectral mapping theorem.

In this talk, I will demonstrate how we can approach this problem in reverse by using the spectral mapping property as part of the definition.  Given a Banach representation M of an algebra B, we will say that a subset of the primitive ideal space of B satisfies this property if it correctly predicts the spectrum of M for commutative subalgebras of B. Then, the projection Taylor spectrum is defined as a unique subset of the primitive ideal space with this property. I will prove that the projection Taylor spectrum exists for representations of finite type algebras and give concrete examples. As an application, I will provide an alternative proof of the Grigorchuk-Yang theorem on the infinite dihedral group.

The talk is based on the extension of my bachelor thesis completed under supervision of A. Yu. Pirkovskii.
